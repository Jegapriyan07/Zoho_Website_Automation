Data Analysts are power users who are responsible for uncovering insights, finding patterns, and trends to support decision-making through in-depth analysis. They're typically part of business or strategy teams. They've a moderate to strong understanding of underlying data models, but may not be technically equipped as a data engineer or scientist.

They often face bottlenecks due to

Fragmented Data Sources

The relevant business data is spread across different software such as ERPs, CRMs, databases, spreadsheets etc

Time-consuming data preparation

Cleaning and combining the disparate raw data is a tedious activity

Limited analytical capabilities

Some tools that are good at basic analytics might not be strong in advanced predictive and generative BI capabilities

Lack of agility

Some BI tools would still require IT support or assistance for creating ad-hoc reports in certain scenarios

Scalability challenges

Managing larger data volumes without compromising performance is an aspect that not BI tools are good at

Data engineers are the backbone of any data analytics operation. They design, build, and manage the data pipelines that ensure data flows from multiple sources in a clean, structured, and reliable manner. Besides that, they take care of data modeling, setting up validation rules, access permissions and everything else focused on performance, scalability and governance.

They often deal with the behind-the-scenes complexity like

Fragmented Data Sources

Data resides in disparate systems. The challenges lie in managing different data formats and brittle data pipelines.

Data Quality Issues

Struggle to enforce standards & validate data from multiple systems results in inconsistent, duplicate, or missing data that hinders reliable insights.

Scalability Constraints

The need to support large datasets and growing user demands brings about performance issues.

Governance Gaps

Difficulty in enforcing security policies, lineage tracking, access controls and compliance with industry-wide standards (like GDPR, SOC 2, ISO/IEC)
