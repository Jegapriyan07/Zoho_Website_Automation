Key features of white label reporting software

Connect to 500+ Platforms

Bring together data from databases, business applications, cloud warehouses, files, feeds, and web URLs into a single reporting environment. Instead of combining information manually across different systems, teams can prepare one data model that supports customer-facing dashboards.

For example, a SaaS provider might combine product usage from its application database, subscription information from Stripe, and support activity from Zendesk to present customers with a unified view of account performance.

Pre-built Reports to Get Started

The platform includes pre-built reports and dashboards that provide a starting point for common reporting scenarios. Teams can customize these templates to match their own data model, branding, and business requirements rather than designing every dashboard from scratch.

For agencies managing multiple client accounts, reusable templates also make it easier to maintain a consistent reporting structure while applying client-specific branding where required.

Your Domain and Branding

Publish dashboards under your own domain or subdomain with your organization's branding applied throughout the experience. Logos, colors, navigation, and other interface elements can be configured to match the surrounding application or customer portal.

This creates a more consistent user experience by presenting analytics as part of your product rather than as a separate third-party service.

Create Your Own Reports

Design reports and dashboards using a drag-and-drop builder that supports a wide range of visualization types. Teams can organize analytical views around different user groups, business functions, or customer segments without developing separate reporting systems.

For example, enterprise customers may require detailed operational dashboards, while smaller customers only need high-level usage summaries.

No-Code and Low-Code Customization

Organizations can extend the platform through configuration options, APIs, and sandbox environments without rebuilding core reporting capabilities. Smaller changes, such as updating layouts or adding calculated metrics, can often be completed through configuration, while APIs provide additional flexibility for product-specific requirements.

This approach allows teams to tailor the analytics experience while continuing to rely on the platform for core reporting infrastructure.

How to Build a White Label Dashboard in Simple Steps

Build vs. Buy White Label Reporting

Organizations can introduce white label reporting either by developing the required capabilities internally or by adopting an existing platform. The choice depends on factors such as engineering capacity, reporting requirements, customization needs, and long-term maintenance responsibilities.

Build In-House

Building internally gives organizations complete control over the reporting infrastructure. Development teams are responsible for creating and maintaining data pipelines, report generation, dashboard rendering, branding, authentication, permissions, and customer-level data isolation. This approach is generally suited to products with highly specialized reporting requirements that cannot be addressed through an existing platform.

Buy a White Label Reporting Platform

A white label reporting platform provides the underlying analytics infrastructure while allowing teams to customize branding, data models, dashboards, and the embedded user experience. Instead of developing core reporting capabilities from scratch, engineering teams can focus on the parts of the product that are unique to their business.

Hybrid Approach

Some organizations combine both approaches by adopting a reporting platform for data connectivity, visualization, security, and embedding while building custom business logic, workflows, and customer-specific experiences on top. This approach reduces infrastructure ownership while preserving flexibility where customization is required.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you remove "Powered by" logos from client reports?

  • Zoho Analytics includes white-label options that allow organizations to replace vendor branding with their own. Depending on the configuration, reports and dashboards can be presented using your logo, domain, colors, and other brand elements so customers experience analytics as part of your product.

Is white label reporting the same as embedded analytics?

  • No. White label reporting focuses on branding, while embedded analytics focuses on where analytics is delivered. With white label reporting, reports and dashboards are presented using your organization's visual identity. Embedded analytics places those dashboards inside another application or customer portal. Many organizations use both together so analytics appears as a branded part of their product experience.

Can white label dashboards support multi-tenant SaaS applications?

  • Yes. Zoho Analytics supports multi-tenant deployments where different customer accounts can access separate dashboards and data from a shared platform. Access can be managed through role-based permissions and customer-level data controls so each account sees only the information available to it while maintaining a consistent branded experience.
