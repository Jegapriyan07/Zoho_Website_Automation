How to Build a White Label Dashboard in Simple Steps

1. Connect Your Data

Start by connecting the data sources you want to report on. Zoho Analytics supports more than 500 connectors, including databases, CRM systems, billing platforms, cloud applications, spreadsheets, and data warehouses, allowing information from different systems to be analyzed together.

2. Prepare the Data Model

Once the data is available, define the relationships between tables and create the calculations your reports depend on. A well-structured data model helps ensure dashboards display consistent metrics across customers and teams.

3. Create the Dashboard

Build reports using the drag-and-drop designer and combine them into dashboards that match how your users consume information. Charts, tables, KPI widgets, filters, and drill-down views can be arranged to support different reporting requirements.

4. Apply Your Brand

Customize the analytics experience with your own logo, colors, navigation, and domain so the dashboards align with the rest of your product or customer portal. This creates a consistent experience for end users without exposing the underlying analytics platform.

5. Configure User Access

Define permissions for different users, teams, or customer accounts. Role-based access and data-level security help ensure each user only sees the information available to them.

6. Publish the Dashboard

The completed dashboard can be embedded into your application or made available through a branded customer portal. Depending on your deployment, dashboards can also be shared securely with internal teams, partners, or external customers.

Build vs. Buy White Label Reporting

Organizations can introduce white label reporting either by developing the required capabilities internally or by adopting an existing platform. The choice depends on factors such as engineering capacity, reporting requirements, customization needs, and long-term maintenance responsibilities. Understanding where the two approaches differ helps teams choose the option that best fits their product and development priorities.

Build In-House

Building internally gives organizations complete control over the reporting infrastructure. Development teams are responsible for creating and maintaining data pipelines, report generation, dashboard rendering, branding, authentication, permissions, and customer-level data isolation. This approach is generally suited to products with highly specialized reporting requirements that cannot be addressed through an existing platform.

Buy a White Label Reporting Platform

A white label reporting platform provides the underlying analytics infrastructure while allowing teams to customize branding, data models, dashboards, and the embedded user experience. Instead of developing core reporting capabilities from scratch, engineering teams can focus on the parts of the product that are unique to their business.

Hybrid Approach

Some organizations combine both approaches by adopting a reporting platform for data connectivity, visualization, security, and embedding while building custom business logic, workflows, and customer-specific experiences on top. This approach reduces infrastructure ownership while preserving flexibility where customization is required.

Frequently Asked Questions (additional questions)

How do you remove "Powered by" logos from client reports?

  • Zoho Analytics includes white-label options that allow organizations to replace vendor branding with their own. Depending on the configuration, reports and dashboards can be presented using your logo, domain, colors, and other brand elements so customers experience analytics as part of your product.

Is white label reporting the same as embedded analytics?

  • No. White label reporting focuses on branding, while embedded analytics focuses on where analytics is delivered. With white label reporting, reports and dashboards are presented using your organization's visual identity. Embedded analytics places those dashboards inside another application or customer portal. Many organizations use both together so analytics appears as a branded part of their product experience.

Can white label dashboards support multi-tenant SaaS applications?

  • Yes. Zoho Analytics supports multi-tenant deployments where different customer accounts can access separate dashboards and data from a shared platform. Access can be managed through role-based permissions and customer-level data controls so each account sees only the information available to it while maintaining a consistent branded experience.
