Finance Dashboard Examples: 10 Templates for CFOs and Finance Teams

Finance dashboards pull data directly from the accounting, billing, and CRM tools your team already uses, and turn it into a live view that updates on its own. Below are 10 finance dashboard examples covering everything from P&L tracking to subscription revenue.

Explore Examples

Finance dashboard examples by type

From cash flow tracking to subscription billing, here are 10 finance dashboard examples you can use as a starting point, depending on what your team needs to track.

1. Finance overview dashboard

Get a complete picture of your financial health in one screen. This dashboard tracks assets, liabilities, revenue, and expenses side by side, with month-over-month comparisons and a detailed financial overview report underneath.

Key KPIs:

  • Total assets
  • Total liabilities
  • Revenue
  • Expenses
  • Current assets
  • Current liabilities
  • Accounts receivable
  • Accounts payable

2. Financial statement dashboard

A structured view of your balance sheet and income data across time periods. Compare year-over-year figures for assets, equity, and liabilities without manually pulling statements from your accounting tool.

Key KPIs:

  • Total assets
  • Total liabilities
  • Total revenue
  • Total expenses
  • Accounts receivable
  • Accounts payable
  • Balance sheet (current year vs prior year)

3. Purchases and payables dashboard

Monitor procurement spend, vendor bills, and outstanding balances over time. This dashboard makes it easy to spot purchase volume trends, track discount utilization, and keep tabs on aging invoices before they become a cash flow problem.

Key KPIs:

  • Total purchases
  • Bills generated
  • Average purchase value
  • Discount %
  • Outstanding balances
  • Purchase trend (actual vs forecast)
  • Bills by status

4. Sales and receivables dashboard

Track invoiced revenue, collections, and overdue accounts in one place. See how sales trend month over month alongside invoice generation rates, and quickly identify where receivables are falling behind.

Key KPIs:

  • Total sales
  • Invoices generated
  • Closed invoices
  • Average sales value
  • Paid invoices
  • Sales trend (actual vs forecast)
  • Invoice status breakdown

5. Subscriptions overview dashboard

Keep a pulse on your subscription business: active customers, new signups, cancellations, and average customer lifetime. This view helps you spot growth slowdowns early and understand which plans are driving retention.

Key KPIs:

  • Active subscriptions
  • Active customers
  • Average customer lifetime
  • Monthly LTV
  • New subscriptions
  • Cancellations
  • New customers this month

6. Revenue overview dashboard

Dig into the revenue numbers behind your subscription growth. Compare MRR year over year, track new versus existing revenue, and monitor churn impact on your recurring revenue baseline.

Key KPIs:

  • YTD revenue
  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
  • New MRR
  • Existing MRR
  • Churned MRR
  • ARPU
  • Revenue growth (this year vs last year)

7. Churn overview dashboard

Understand exactly where and when you're losing customers. Track churn rate, cancellations, and MRR lost month by month, so your team can act before retention problems compound.

Key KPIs:

  • Customer churn
  • Churn rate
  • Cancellations
  • MRR lost on cancellations
  • Retention rate
  • Monthly churn trend

8. Executive overview dashboard

A high-level view built for leadership. This dashboard combines customer count, subscription activity, revenue, and churn into one clean screen, so executives can assess business health without digging into multiple reports.

Key KPIs:

  • Total customers
  • Active subscriptions
  • Paid invoice rate
  • Churn rate
  • Total revenue
  • MRR
  • ARPU
  • Monthly revenue trend
  • Customer growth trend

9. Revenue and billing intelligence dashboard

Go deeper than top-line revenue. This dashboard breaks down collected revenue, outstanding balances, net revenue, and invoice aging, giving your finance team full visibility into billing performance and collection efficiency.

Key KPIs:

  • Revenue generated
  • Outstanding balance
  • Net revenue
  • MRR
  • Revenue collected
  • Gross collection rate
  • Average invoice value
  • Invoice aging report

10. Payments performance and pricing insights dashboard

Track payment collection success, failure rates, and transaction patterns across your billing infrastructure. Identify which payment methods and gateways perform best, and where failed payments are costing you revenue.

Key KPIs:

  • Collection success rate
  • Collection failure rate
  • Average payment attempts per invoice
  • Average transaction value
  • Gross refund amount
  • Payment method split
  • Failure reason distribution

FAQs

What is a finance dashboard?

  • A finance dashboard is a live visual report that pulls data from your accounting tools and displays key metrics, such as revenue, expenses, cash flow, and liabilities, in one place. It replaces the need to manually compile numbers from multiple sources every time someone needs an update.

What should a finance dashboard include?

  • Total revenue, total expenses, net profit, cash flow, and accounts receivable versus payable are the core metrics. Some finance teams also add budget versus actual, profit margin, and period-over-period comparisons depending on what they report on regularly.

Who uses finance dashboards?

  • CFOs and finance managers use them for oversight and reporting. Accountants use them to track transactions and reconcile numbers. Business owners use them to monitor cash flow and profitability without waiting for month-end reports.

Can I build a finance dashboard without technical skills?

  • Yes. Zoho Analytics connects directly to your accounting tools and offers prebuilt templates you can use right away. You can also use its AI capabilities, like Ask Zia, to create dashboards using simple prompts and get insights with Zia Insights.

How do I connect my accounting software to Zoho Analytics?

  • Go to the data sources section, select your tool, such as Zoho Books, QuickBooks, Xero, or Stripe, and authorize the connection. Your data starts syncing automatically from there.

Can I combine data from multiple finance tools in one dashboard?

  • Yes. Zoho Analytics lets you pull data from several sources at once. For example, you can connect Zoho Books for accounting and Stripe for payments and blend them into a single dashboard without any manual work. You can also blend in non-financial apps, like a CRM, for a more complete analysis.

Can I schedule finance reports to be sent automatically?

  • Yes. Zoho Analytics lets you set up automated email reports on a daily, weekly, or monthly schedule. Your CFO or board members receive the latest numbers in their inbox without anyone having to manually pull and send them.

Are the finance dashboard templates free to use?
