What key features to look for in a client dashboard tool?

A client dashboard should do two things. It has to make reporting faster for your team, and make your work obvious to the client. When you're comparing tools, these are the features that matter once you're managing more than a handful of accounts.

One source of truth across every channel

The fastest way to lose a retainer is a client's finance team catching two different numbers for the same metric. A client dashboard tool should connect all your sources and reconcile them into one set of numbers everyone agrees on.

White-label branding

A dashboard that carries a third-party vendor's name quietly undercuts the value you're charging for. Clients should open something that looks like it came from you. Look for full white-labeling, not just a logo upload.

Live dashboards clients can drill into

Clients don't want to schedule a call to ask why a number moved, and you don't want to field that email. A static PDF forces both. A live dashboard lets the client open their numbers whenever they want and click into the detail themselves.

Customizable to each client's goals

A PPC client and a brand client don't measure success the same way, so a single generic template fails both. The tool should let you tailor what each client sees to the KPIs they actually care about.

AI that explains the numbers

Clients pay for the story around the data. What happened, why, and what to do next. A tool that surfaces that automatically saves you from writing it up every month, and you can hand the AI to clients directly.

Automated, scheduled reporting

Pulling data and rebuilding the same report by hand is the cost agencies feel most, often hours per client every month. The whole point of a dashboard tool is to remove that.
